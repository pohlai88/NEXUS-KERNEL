Problem & Context

Page 2

What is happening today

  • The design system lacks a single enforceable standard, leading to inconsistent UI decisions across modules and teams.
  • Patterns, tokens, and interaction states are not consistently applied, increasing one-off implementations.

Why it matters

Delivery velocity

Repeated UI decisions slow delivery and increase rework as the component surface area expands.

Executive trust

Uneven quality undermines confidence in the platform and weakens premium perception.

Governance cost

Drift increases long-term maintenance, review overhead, and refactoring frequency.

Brand authority

Inconsistent interaction patterns dilute “one system” feel—critical for enterprise adoption.

So what: A single standard must be visually authoritative and operationally enforceable (tokens + patterns + state rules), not merely guidelines.

What happens if we do nothing

  • Delivery velocity declines as the system grows and teams repeat the same decisions.
  • Governance overhead rises (more reviews, more refactors, more exceptions).
  • Professional perception varies by module, weakening enterprise adoption.

Recommendation

Page 5

Recommended option

Adopt Quantum Obsidian as the system-wide standard.

Why this wins

  • Authority & trust: disciplined neutrals and restrained chroma signal institutional confidence.
  • Enforceability: semantic tokens + patterns + unified states reduce drift by design.

Tradeoffs accepted

  • Prioritize trust and governance over maximum visual experimentation.
  • Restrict accent usage to preserve clarity in data-heavy dashboards.

Implementation directive

  • Apply Quantum Obsidian to all new components immediately.
